About Me

Hi! As you may have guessed my name is Sam Eskandar and I am a Mechatronics Engineering student at the University of Waterloo with a passion for Software ad Web Development.

Although I spend most of my time in class solving integrals or behind a computer writing various list comprehensions in python, it wasn't always like that (okay maybe the integral part was)!

I used to be sure of myself pursuing a Medical degree and becoming a doctor as many of my uncles are Doctors and my two of my cousins are in Medical School along with my sister (crazy huh!). However, when I wrote my first python program (#HelloWorld) I realized that although Doctors are amazing individuals helping the sick and tackling modern diseases head on, Engineers are constantly building creative solutions to many issues facing the Medical World today! For example the MRI is an amazing display of how Engineers are constantly contributing to the Medical Field. It was this reason that I decided to pursue an Engineering degree, as so many technologies that I will contribute to in the future will directly affect its users in a positive way.

Another reason why I love Engineering is how it highlights the importance of teamwork. One of my favourite videos that inspires me on a daily basis and really outlines the dedication Engineers put into their projects and careers can be found here.

However, Engineering and school is not everything I am. I also am a musician! I have been playing the trumpet for 6 years now and for the past four have been lead trumpet for the Toronto All-Star Big Band and Etobicoke Jazz Band (TABB's sister organization).

With this position I have played at various venues such as the Beaches Jazz Festival, Mel Lastman Square, and Parry Sounds Festival of the Sound. In addition to the concerts I've played at, I've made amazing memories and connections with not only my bandmates but the numerous individuals I had the chance to meet and talk to through many performances at Retirement Homes around the Etobicoke area.

If you wanted to hear me play here's a video of the band performing Michael Buble's rendition of "Save the Last Dance For Me" at Mel Lastman Square (if you are having a hard time finding me, I'm the shortest trumpet player playing the unnecessarily high notes).
